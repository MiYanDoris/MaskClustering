MaskClustering: View Consensus based Mask Graph Clustering
for Open-Vocabulary 3D Instance Segmentation

1Peking University    2Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence    3Galbot   

† corresponding author

Abstract

Open-vocabulary 3D instance segmentation has emerged as a frontier topic due to its capability to segment 3D instances beyond a predefined set of categories. However, compared to significant progress in the 2D domain, methods for 3D open-vocabulary instance segmentation are hindered by the limited scale of high-quality annotated 3D data. To capitalize on the advantages of the 2D domain, we propose to cluster view-consensus 2D masks with corresponding open-vocabulary features for open-vocabulary 3D instance segmentation. Our method evaluates view consensus of all candidate mask pairs, which indicates that both two masks should be contained in a substantial number of other observed masks. Mask pairs are represented as two connected nodes of a mask graph, and the weight of the edge is the view consensus, then our method iterative clusters the nodes with shared high view consensus. The corresponding 3D points cluster of the 2D mask clusters can be regarded as zero-shot 3D instances, along with the fused open-vocabulary features from clustered 2D masks. The advantages of our methods come from the prior instance knowledge from massive zero-shot masks predicted by visual foundation models, without the need of training on 3D data. Through extensive experiments on publicly available datasets, including ScanNet200 and MatterPort3D, we demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in both zero-shot segmentation and open-vocabulary understanding.
